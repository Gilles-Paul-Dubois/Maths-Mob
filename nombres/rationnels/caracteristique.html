Definition

Let (K,+,×) be a with 1.
Consider for n ∈ ℤ, the set of elements of K of the form n1 (multiples of 1 within the meaning of ).
One of two possibilities happens:
  • Either ∃ n ∈ ℤ* | n1=0
  • Or ∀ n ∈ ℤ* n1 ≠ 0
Consider the first case and let p be the smallest integer p >0 such that p1=0. Then p is necessarily prime. Indeed, otherwise we have p=kh and (k1)(h1)=0 ⇒ k1=0 or h1=0 because there is no in a field, what contradicts the definition of p.
definition
In this case we say that 'K is of characteristic p' .
Otherwise, we say that 'K is of characteristic 0' .
Let us now denote by P the smallest subfield of K. i.e. the smallest of of K containing unity, for the operation × and such that for any element x of P the of x is still an element of P.
In the case of characteristic 0, it is clear that P must contain the numbers of the form m.1 as well as their inverses of the form 1/n1 and the products of such elements therefore the elements of the form m1/n1. Then the map m/n → m1/n1 from ℚ to K is an from ℚ onto P.
