Definitions

Let n be a nonzero natural integer and m any natural integer.
The intervals [kn, (k+1)n[, determined by the of n, form a of ℕ.
m is therefore in one of them and in only one, say [qn, (q+1)n[.
In other words q is the greatest of the integers k such that kn ≤ m..
This remark being made, the number r=m-qn is necessarily <n.
definition
q is called the 'quotient' and r the 'remainder' of the 'Euclidean division' of m by n.
m=nq+r q,r ∈ ℕ r < n
Intuitively q represents How many times you can place n in m , and r is what remains given that in general m is not a multiple of n.
(m multiple of n) ⇔ (r=0).
