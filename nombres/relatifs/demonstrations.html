Here are now some results, already used in the chapter dedicated to the natural numbers, but the proof of which we have postponed because it becomes remarkably simple with Bézout's identity.

Gauss's theorem

theorem
a,b,c denoting three nonzero relative integers if a divides bc and is prime with b then a divides c.

proof

Let's start from ua+bv=1 let's multiply by c uac+bcv=c since uac is divisible by a and bcv also since bc is we get the desired result.
Carl Friedrich Gauss

Théorème d'Euclide

théorème
p étant un nombre premier si p divise ab alors p divise a ou p divise b.

démonstration

C'est une conséquence du précédent sachant que si p est premier et n quelconque non nul, on a les deux seules possibilités pgcd(n,p)=p ou pgcd(n,p)=1

Equations diophantiennes

théorème
a,b,c étant trois entiers relatifs non nuls, une condition nécessaire et suffisante pour qu'il existe des solutions entières à:
ax+by=c
est que c soit un multiple de pgcd(a,b).
C'est une conséquence immédiate de l'identité de Bézout.
En outre pour la résolution on procéde comme suit.
Soit d=pgcd(a,b) on isole un couple de Bézout (u,v) pour l'équation
ax+by=d
Ensuite on doit avoir c=kd donc on prend comme solution particulière (ku,kv).
Il suffit alors d'ajouter à cette solution particulière la solution générale de l'équation homogène associée:
ax+by=0

Euclid's theorem

theorem
p being a prime number if p divides ab then p divides a or p divides b.

proof

This is a consequence of the previous knowing that if p is prime and n any nonzero, we have the only two possibilities gcd(n,p)=p or gcd(n,p)=1

This is now an immediate consequence of Euclid's theorem.

Diophantine equations

theorem
a,b,c being three nonzero relative integers, a necessary condition and sufficient that there exist integer solutions to:
ax+by=c
is that c is a multiple of gcd(a,b).
This is an immediate consequence of Bézout's identity.
In addition for the resolution one proceeds as follows.
Let d=gcd(a,b) we isolate a Bézout couple (u,v) for the equation
ax+by=d
Then we must have c=kd so we take as a particular solution (ku, kv).
It is then enough to add to this particular solution the general solution of the associated homogeneous equation:
ax+by=0
