Dans l'espace vectoriel ℝ3, on considère l'application <.,.> :

\( \displaystyle \left ( \overrightarrow{u}(x,y,z),\overrightarrow{v}(x',y',z') \right ) \mapsto \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{v} \right \rangle= (x-2y)(x'-2y')+yy'+(y+z)(y'+z') \)

Montrer que <.,.> est un produit scalaire.

aide

En appliquant la définition, montrer que <.,.> est bilinéaire, symétrique définie positive.

solution

<.,.> est la somme de 3 fonctions qui sont des produits de formes linéaires.
<.,.> est donc bilinéaire comme somme de formes bilinéaires.
<.,.> est évidemment symétrique.

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle= (x-2y)(x-2y)+yy+(y+z)(y+z)=(x-2y)^{2}+y^{2}+(y+z)^{2} \)

Donc

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle=0 \Leftrightarrow (x-2y)^{2}=0 ~~et~~y^{2}=0~~et~~(y+z)^{2}=0 \)

soit

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle=0 \Leftrightarrow (x-2y)=0 ~~et~~y=0~~et~~(y+z)=0 \)

Le système

\( \displaystyle \left\{ \begin{matrix} {x = 2y} \\ {y = 0} \\ {y + z = 0} \\ \end{matrix} \right. \)

possède pour seule solution x=y=z=0.

In the vector space ℝ3, we consider the map <.,.> :

\( \displaystyle \left ( \overrightarrow{u}(x,y,z),\overrightarrow{v}(x',y',z') \right ) \mapsto \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{v} \right \rangle= (x-2y)(x'-2y')+yy'+(y+z)(y'+z') \)

Show that <.,.> is a dot product.

hint

By applying the definition, show that <.,.> is bilinear, symmetric positive definite.

solution

<.,.> is the sum of 3 functions which are products of linear forms.
<.,.> is therefore bilinear as a sum of bilinear forms.
<.,.> is obviously symmetrical.

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle= (x-2y)(x-2y)+yy+(y+z)(y+z)=(x-2y)^{2}+y^{2}+(y+z)^{2} \)

So

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle=0 \Leftrightarrow (x-2y)^{2}=0 ~~and~~y^{2}=0~~and~~(y+z)^{2}=0 \)

or

\( \displaystyle \left \langle \overrightarrow{u},\overrightarrow{u} \right \rangle=0 \Leftrightarrow (x-2y)=0 ~~and~~y=0~~and~~(y+z)=0 \)

The system

\( \displaystyle \left\{ \begin{matrix} {x=2y} \\ {y=0} \\ {y+z=0} \\ \end{matrix} \right. \)

has for only solution x=y=z=0.
