Définition

Soit P et Q deux points distincts d'un espace affine euclidien A de dimension finie.
Nous cherchons l'ensemble (ou encore 'lieu géométrique' selon une terminologie ancienne) des points M 'équidistants' de P et Q, c'est à dire situés à même distance de P que de Q.
Cherchons à résoudre ce problème analytiquement, en introduisant un repère R.
Dans ce repère le point P a des coordonnées (a1, ... ,an) et le point Q des coordonnées (b1, ... ,bn).

d(M,P)=d(M,Q) ⇔ d(M,P)2=d(M,Q)2

Definition

Let P and Q be two distinct points of a finite dimensional Euclidean affine space A.
We are looking for the set (or still 'geometric locus' according to old terminology) of points M 'equidistant' of P and Q, i.e. located at the same distance from P as from Q.
Let's try to solve this problem analytically, by introducing a benchmark R.
In this frame, point P has coordinates (a1, ... ,an) and point Q has coordinates (b1 , ... ,bn).
The distances being positive numbers we have:
d(M,P)=d(M,Q) ⇔ d(M,P)2=d(M,Q)2
By translating this last equality by means of the coordinates we obtain, if the xi are the coordinates of M:

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{n}\left( x_{i} - a_{i} \right)^{2}} = {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{n }\left( {x_{i} - b_{i}} \right)^{2}} \)

By expanding and eliminating the squares we get the Cartesian equation of a hyperplane, because there exists at least one index i such that ai ≠ bi since P ≠ Q.
It is obvious that this hyperplane passes through the middle I of the segment [PQ].
The triangle MPQ is then isosceles with vertex M and (MI) is a median of it, therefore also a height and (MI) is orthogonal to [PQ].
It follows that :
propriété 1
Le lieu des points équidistants de deux points distincts P et Q est un hyperplan passant par le milieu de [PQ] et orthogonal à [PQ].
property 1
The locus of points equidistant from two distinct points P and Q is a hyperplane passing through the midpoint of [PQ] and orthogonal to [PQ].
définition 1
Cet hyperplan s'appelle l'hyperplan 'médiateur' de [PQ].
definition 1
This hyperplane is called the 'perpendicular bisector' hyperplane of [PQ].
