Examples

  • The identical application is a proper isometry.
  • Translations are proper isometrys.
  • In the plane, a point reflection is a proper isometry.
  • In 3-dimensional space a point reflection is not a proper isometry.
Intuitively, two plane isometric figures correspond by a proper isometry if one can make them coincide with each other by operations of the 'sliding' (translation) and 'rotation' type (we will come back in detail to plane rotations).
