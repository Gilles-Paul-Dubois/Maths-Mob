On considère l'espace vectoriel réel A=ℝ3 muni de sa structure affine naturelle.
On considère dans A le plan P1 de vecteurs directeurs :
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{1}}=(1,-1,1) \)
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{1}}=(0,1,0) \)
passant par M1(0,0,0).
Ce plan est-il parallèle au plan P2 de vecteurs directeurs :
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}}=(2,-2,2) \)
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}}=(1,0,1) \)
passant par M2(1,-1,1).
Les deux plans sont-ils strictement parallèles ?

aide

On remarquera que \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}} \) et \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}} \) sont combinaisons linéaires de \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{1}} \) et \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{1}} \) .

solution

On a :
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}} = 2\overrightarrow{u_{1}} \)
et
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}} = \overrightarrow{u_{1}}+\overrightarrow{v_{1}} \)
Les deux plans sont donc parallèles.
il est clair que M2 ∈ P1 car \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{M_{1}M_{2}}=\overrightarrow{u_{1}} \)
Les plans sont donc confondus.

We consider the real vector space A=ℝ3 endowed with its natural affine structure.
We consider in A the plane P1 of direction vectors:
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{1}}=(1,-1,1) \)
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{1}}=(0,1,0) \)
passing through M1(0,0,0).
Is this plane parallel to the P2 plane of direction vectors:
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}}=(2,-2,2) \)
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}}=(1,0,1) \)
passing through M2(1,-1,1).
Are the two planes strictly parallel?

hint

Note that \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}} \) and \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}} \) are linear combinations of \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{1}} \) and \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{1}} \) .

solution

We have:
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{u_{2}} = 2\overrightarrow{u_{1}} \)
and
\( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{v_{2}} = \overrightarrow{u_{1}}+\overrightarrow{v_{1}} \)
The two planes are therefore parallel.
it is clear that M2 ∈ P1 because \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{M_{1}M_{2}}=\overrightarrow{u_{1}} \)
The planes are therefore the same.
