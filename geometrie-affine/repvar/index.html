Le but est de faire la même chose avec les points d'un espace affine.
Dans la mesure où tout espace affine est en bijection avec un espace vectoriel, dans le cas de la dimension finie, les vecteurs pouvant être représentés par des n-uples au moyen du choix d'une base, il en sera de même pour les points.

This purely technical chapter does not involve any major difficulty. It only assumes known the regarding the definition of affine spaces and linear varieties as well as what concerns .
We take up the idea of representing vectors by n-tuples of numbers.
The goal is to do the same thing with the points of an affine space.
Insofar as any affine space is in bijection with a vector space, in the case of the finite dimension, the vectors being able to be represented by n-tuples by means of the choice of a basis, it will be the same for the points .
However the bijection between A the affine space and E its associated vector space depends on the choice of a particular point called 'origin'. Thus the correspondence point ↔ n-tuple will depend partly on the choice of the origin and partly on the choice of a basis.
The bijection being established, we will seek to characterize linear varieties (and later other sets) by equations or systems of equations which are in fact equations in a multi-dimensional space.
We remind that throughout this chapter and other ones the terms 'linear variety', 'linear manifold' and 'affine subspace' will be considered as synonymous.
The fact that any manifold can be seen as the intersection of affine hyperplanes will play a special role. The varieties will then appear as the solutions of linear systems, and since the solutions of such systems have already been extensively studied in linear algebra, one can reuse known results.
Parametric representations are the application of the above principle (bijection with a power of K) to linear varieties which can be considered as affine spaces in their own right. Thus for a linear manifold B of dimension p in an affine space of dimension n, any point of B can be bijected (in several ways) with a p-uple of elements of K (base field).
We will then try to detect the parallelism of the varieties on the analytical representations of these varieties (Cartesian equations or parametric representations), and to characterize the intersection of varieties represented by systems of equations or parametric equations, either by a new system of Cartesian equations or by a new parametric representation.
