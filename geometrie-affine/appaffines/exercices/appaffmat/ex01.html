Soit A l'espace vectoriel ℝ3 muni de sa structure affine naturelle.
On suppose donné dans A un repère affine \( \displaystyle R=\left ( O, \overrightarrow{i} ,\overrightarrow{j},\overrightarrow{k}\right ) \) .
Donner dans ce repère l'expression analytique des 3 applications affines suivantes :

  1. L'homothétie de centre C(1,2,3) et de rapport 4.
  2. La projection sur le plan x+y+z=1 parallèlement au vecteur \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \)
  3. L'affinité de base le plan x+y+z=1 parallèlement au vecteur \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \)

aide

  1. Ecrire que \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{CM'}=4\overrightarrow{CM} \)
  2. Ecrire que \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{MM'} \) est colinéaire à \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \) et que M' appartient au plan x+y+z=1
  3. Si M" est le transformé de M, et si M' est son projeté, écrire que \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{M'M''}=3\overrightarrow{M'M} \)

solution

  1. x'=4x-3
    y'=4y-6
    z'=4z-9
  2. x'=1-y-z
    y'=y
    z'=z
  3. x'=3x+2y+2z-2
    y'=y
    z'=z

Let A be the vector space ℝ3 endowed with its natural affine structure.
We suppose given in A an affine frame \( \displaystyle R=\left ( O, \overrightarrow{i} ,\overrightarrow{j},\overrightarrow{k}\right ) \) .
Give in this frame the analytical expression of the following 3 affine maps:

  1. The point reflection with center C(1,2,3) and ratio 4.
  2. The projection on the plane x+y+z=1 parallel to the vector \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \)
  3. Basic stretching with respect to the plane x+y+z=1 parallel to the vector \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \)

hint

  1. Write that \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{CM'}=4\overrightarrow{CM} \)
  2. Write that \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{MM'} \) is collinear with \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{i} \) and that M' belongs to the plane x+y+z=1
  3. If M" is the transform of M, and if M' is its projection, write that \( \displaystyle \overrightarrow{M'M''}=3\overrightarrow{M'M} \)

solution

  1. x'=4x-3
    y'=4y-6
    z'=4z-9
  2. x'=1-y-z
    y'=y
    z'=z
  3. x'=3x+2y+2z-2
    y'=y
    z'=z
