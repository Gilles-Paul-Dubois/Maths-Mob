We are interested here in the particular case of dimension 2.
We therefore study the Euclidean affine space ℝ2. We will first see that displacements and antidisplacements are easy to characterize.
The two following sentences concern mainly the French educational system (when it still existed...)
In old French curricula, let's say before the Lichnerowicz reform, angles were not formally defined but they were used extensively.
After this reform the angles were painstakingly defined, but nothing was done about them.
We try here to give a 'reasonable' definition of angles and their measures and to show that this notion is useful for geometric reasoning, visualized by 'figures'.
