Démontrez le théorème de Gergonne qui affirme que si (CP),(AQ) et (BR) sont concourantes en M, alors :

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{MP}}{\overline{CP}} + \frac{\overline{MQ}}{\overline{AQ}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = 1 \)

aide

Appliquer Ménélaüs pour le triangle MRC et la sécante (PAB).
Appliquer Ménélaüs encore pour le triangle AMR et la sécante (BQC).

solution

Appliquons Ménélaüs pour le triangle MRC et la sécante (PAB).

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{PM}}{\overline{PC}} \times \frac{\overline{AC}}{\overline{AR}} \times \frac{\overline{BR}}{\overline{BM}} = 1 \)

Appliquons Ménélaüs encore pour le triangle AMR et la sécante (BQC).

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{QM}}{\overline{QA}} \times \frac{\overline{CA}}{\overline{CR}} \times \frac{\overline{BR}}{\overline{BM}} = 1 \)

D'où nous tirons :

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{PM}}{\overline{PC}} = \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} \)

et

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{QM}}{\overline{QA}} = \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline{CA}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} \)

D'où en remplaçant :

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{MP}}{\overline{CP}} + \frac{\overline{MQ}}{\overline{AQ}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline{CA}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} \times \left( \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} + \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline{CA}} \right) + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = 1 \)

Prove Gergonne's theorem which states that if (CP),(AQ) and (BR) are concurrent in M, then:

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{MP}}{\overline{CP}} + \frac{\overline{MQ}}{\overline{AQ}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR }} = 1 \)

hint

Apply Menelaus for triangle MRC and secant (PAB).
Apply Menelaus again for triangle AMR and secant (BQC).

solution

Let's apply Menelaus for the triangle MRC and the secant (PAB).

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{PM}}{\overline{PC}} \times \frac{\overline{AC}}{\overline{AR}} \times \frac{\overline{BR}}{\overline {BM}} = 1 \)

Let's apply Menelaus again for the triangle AMR and the secant (BQC).

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{QM}}{\overline{QA}} \times \frac{\overline{CA}}{\overline{CR}} \times \frac{\overline{BR}}{\overline {BM}} = 1 \)

From where we draw:

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{PM}}{\overline{PC}} = \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{ BR}} \)

and

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{QM}}{\overline{QA}} = \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline{CA}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{ BR}} \)

Hence by replacing:

\( \displaystyle \frac{\overline{MP}}{\overline{CP}} + \frac{\overline{MQ}}{\overline{AQ}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR }} = \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline {CA}} \times \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = \frac{\overline{BM}}{ \overline{BR}} \times \left( \frac{\overline{AR}}{\overline{AC}} + \frac{\overline{CR}}{\overline{CA}} \right) + \frac {\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = \frac{\overline{BM}}{\overline{BR}} + \frac{\overline{MR}}{\overline{BR}} = 1 \)
