Definition

definition
Among all of E (finite or not) some may exist with 2, so they are their own inverses, we call them 'involutions' or equivalently 'involutory or self-inverse mappings' .

Examples

For example, geometrical transformations which are named symmetries relative to a point (center) a straight line (axis) or a plane are self_inverses.
