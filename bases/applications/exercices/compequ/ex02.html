Let f be compatible with R and S.
Let X be the class of x modulo R
Let y=f(x) and Y the class of y
Let us show that Y does not depend on the representative x chosen in X.
Indeed if x' is another representative of X and if y'=f(x') we have xRx' then f(x)Sf(x')
That is to say that the class of f(y) and that of f(y') are the same.
Let us assume, under these conditions, g(X)=Y we thus define a map E/R → F/S possessing the desired property.

Conversely, suppose that g exists g:E/R → F/S such that gop=qof Then if xRx' x and x' have the same class X modulo R. We have
g(p(x))=q(f(x))=g(X)
and
g(p(x')=q(f(x')=g(X)
So f(x) and f(x') have the same class Y modulo S.
