Definition

definition
In the case of finite sets (of small size), a can also be represented in another way by means of a so-called 'sagittal' diagram.
This word comes from a Latin word meaning 'arrow' (we also say 'arrow diagram').
For this we represent vertically the two sets, the departure on the left and the destination on the right. Each time an ordered pair (x, y) is in the graph we draw an arrow from x to y.

Arrow diagrams are not suitable for representing large graphs, but they have the advantage of allowing a good mental representation (visualization) of certain concepts.

For some notions the Cartesian graph is more suitable, for others the sagittal diagram is more intuitive.
