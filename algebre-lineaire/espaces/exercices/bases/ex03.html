Let K be a field. E denotes the vector space of polynomials with coefficients in K of degree ≤ 1. Show that if K= ℚ where K= ℝ then E admits (X+1, X-1) as a basis.

hint

Review the of a field.

solution

We have 1=((X+1)-(X-1))/2 and X= ((X+1)+(X-1))/2 .
So any vector aX+b can be written a((X+1)+(X-1))/2 + b((X+1)-(X-1))/2.
(X-1,X+1) therefore spans E.
Moreover a(X+1)+b(X-1) = 0 ⇒ a+b=0 and ab=0 so a=b=0.
If K is a field of characteristic 2, for example K= ℤ/2ℤ then we have (X+1)+(X-1) =0 so (X-1,X+1) is not linearly independent and therefore is not a basis.
