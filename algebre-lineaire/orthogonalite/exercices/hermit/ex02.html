On appelle 'Trace' d'une matrice carrée la somme de ses éléments diagonaux.
Si A=(αi,j) est d'ordre n
Tr(A)= α1,1+α2,2+...+αn,n
Soit E l'espace vectoriel M(n,ℂ) des matrices carrées d'ordre n à coefficients complexes.
Si A et B ∈ E, on pose:
<A,B>=Tr(A*B)
où A* est la transposée de la matrice conjuguée de A, c'est à dire si A=(αi,j) alors B=(βi,j) où \(\beta _{i,j}=\overline{\alpha _{j,i}}\)
Montrer que l'application (A,B) → <A,B> est sesquilinéaire hermitienne.

We call 'Trace' of a square matrix the sum of its diagonal elements.
If A=(αi,j) is of order n
Tr(A)= α1.1+α2.2+...+αn,n
Let E be the vector space M(n,ℂ) of square matrices of order n with complex coefficients.
If A and B ∈ E, we set:
<A,B>=Tr(A*B)
where A* is the transpose of the conjugate matrix of A, i.e. if A=(αi,j) then B=(βi,j) where \(\beta _{i,j}=\overline{\alpha _{j,i}}\)

solution

The solution is very similar to the .
The only difference is to show that <B,A> = <A,B>
But \( \text{<}B,A\text{>} =Tr\left ( B^{\ast }A \right ) =Tr\left ( \left ( B^{\ast }A \right )^{T} \right )=Tr\left ( A^{T}\left ( B^{\ast } \right )^{T} \right )\)
And we have Tr( M )= Tr(M) which gives :
\(Tr\left ( \overline{A^{T}B^{\ast T}} \right )=\overline{Tr\left ( A^{T}B^{\ast T} \right )}\)
and finally <B,A> = <A,B>.
