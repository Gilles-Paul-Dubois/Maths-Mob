definition
We call 'image' of the linear application f: E → F the subspace of F defined by f(E)= {v ∈ F | ∃ u ∈ E v=f(u)}.
The image of f is traditionally denoted 'Im(f)'.

Property

The following property results from the definition of a surjection:
f surjective ⇔ Im(f)=F

Examples

  1. If we take for space the polynomials of degree ≤ n, and for map D: p → p' (derived polynomial). Im(f) is the set of polynomials of degree ≤ n-1.
  2. If E= Kn F=K and p the first projection p(x1,...,xn)=x1 Im(p)=K.
  3. The image of u → λu is either {0} if λ=0 or E if λ ≠ 0.
