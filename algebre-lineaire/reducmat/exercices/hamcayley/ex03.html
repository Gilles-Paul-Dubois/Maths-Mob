\( \begin{pmatrix} a& 1 & 0 & . & 0 \\ 0 & a & 1 & . & 0 \\ . & . & .& . & . \\ 0 & 0 & . & a & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & . & a \\ \end{pmatrix} \)

Show that its minimal polynomial () is (X-a)n

hint

Start by studying the case n=2 .

solution

In the case n=2. The minimal polynomial is either (X-a) or (X-a)2 . It cannot be (X-a) because the map would then be a dilation. It is therefore (X-a)2 .
In the general case, we can reason by induction.
The result is established for n=1 and 2.
Suppose it is established up to order n ≥ 2. Let A be a matrix

\( \begin{pmatrix} a& 1 & 0 & . & 0 \\ 0 & a & 1 & . & 0 \\ . & . & .& . & . \\ 0 & 0 & . & a & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & . & a \\ \end{pmatrix} \)

of order n+1 ≥ 3
We can then write:

\( A=\left( \begin{array}{c|c} A' & B \\ \hline 0 & A'' \end{array} \right) \)

with

\( A'= \begin{pmatrix} a& 1\\ 0 & a \\ \end{pmatrix} \)

and A" is of the same type as A of order n-1.
