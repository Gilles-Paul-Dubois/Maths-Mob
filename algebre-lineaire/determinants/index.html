In practice most of the results of linear algebra contribute to solve the systems of equations of the first degree, aptly called 'linear systems'.
The theory starts with deciding of the existence of solutions and their form. This preparatory work is done by means of a rank calculation.
The theory of determinants finds its origin in the search for a process of systematic calculation making it possible to establish the rank of a system, predict whether a square matrix is ​​invertible and calculate its inverse.
For dimension 2, the results are simple and known for a long time.

It will therefore be necessary to choose other heuristics.
