Soit Ω=ℕ*={1,2, ...,n, ...}.
Soit p un réel p ∈ ]0,1[.

On pose :

\( \displaystyle p_{i} = P\left( \left\{ i \right\} \right) = p\left( {1 - p} \right)^{i - 1} \)

Montrer qu'on obtient ainsi une distribution de probabilité sur Ω, appelée 'distribution géométrique' ou 'distribution de Pascal' de paramètre p.
Calculer P({1,2, ... ,n} et P({m,m+1, ... ,n}) (m≤n).

aide

Il suffit de montrer que

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{\infty}p_{i}} = 1 \)

solution

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{\infty}p_{i}} = p{\sum\limits_{i = 0}^{\infty}q^{n}} \)

où q=(1-p)

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 0}^{\infty}q^{n} = \frac{1}{1 - q}} = \frac{1}{1 - \left( {1 - p} \right)} = \frac{1}{p} \)

\( \displaystyle P\left( \left\{ 1,2,{...},n \right\} \right) = p\left( {1 + q + q^{2} + ... + q^{n}} \right) = p\frac{1 - q^{n}}{1 - q} = 1 - q^{n} \)

\( \displaystyle P\left( \left\{ m,{m + 1},{...},n \right\} \right) = \left( {1 - q^{n}} \right) - \left( {1 - q^{m}} \right) = q^{m} - q^{n} \)

Let Ω=ℕ*={1,2, ...,n, ...}.
Let p be a real p ∈ ]0.1[.

We put:

\( \displaystyle p_{i} = P\left( \left\{ i \right\} \right) = p\left( {1 - p} \right)^{i - 1} \)

Show that we thus obtain a probability distribution on Ω, called 'geometric distribution' or 'Pascal's distribution' of parameter p.
Calculate P({1,2, ... ,n} and P({m,m+1, ... ,n}) (m≤n).

hint

Just show that :

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{\infty}p_{i}} = 1 \)

solution

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{\infty}p_{i}} = p{\sum\limits_{i = 0}^{\infty}q^{n}} \)

where q=(1-p)

\( \displaystyle {\sum\limits_{i = 0}^{\infty}q^{n} = \frac{1}{1 - q}} = \frac{1}{1 - \left( {1 - p} \right)} = \frac{1}{p} \)

\( \displaystyle P\left( \left\{ 1,2,{...},n \right\} \right) = p\left( {1 + q + q^{2} + ... + q^{n}} \right) = p\frac{1 - q^{n}}{1 - q} = 1 - q^{n} \)

\( \displaystyle P\left( \left\{ m,{m + 1},{...},n \right\} \right) = \left( {1 - q^{n}} \right) - \left( {1 - q^{m}} \right) = q^{m} - q^{n} \)
