Montrer que la série de fonctions de terme général un(x)=nxn converge simplement sur ]-1,+1[ et sur aucun ensemble strictement plus grand.

aide

Utiliser le .

solution

un+1(x)/un(x)=(1+1/n)x
Comme limn→∞(1+1/n)=1 on a limn→∞un+1(x)/un(x)=x.
D'où la convergence de la série de terme général un(x) si |x|<1.
Si |x|≥1 la série ne peut converger car son terme général ne tend pas vers 0, il tend vers +∞ si x≥1 et il oscille infiniment si x≤-1.

Show that the series of functions with general term un(x)=nxn simply converges on ]-1,+1[ and on no set strictly bigger.

hint

Use .

solution

un+1(x)/un(x)=(1+1/n)x
As limn→∞(1+1/n)=1 we have limn→∞un+1(x) /un(x)=x.
Hence the convergence of the series of general term un(x) if |x| <1.
If |x|≥1 the series cannot converge because its general term does not tend to 0, it tends to +∞ if x≥1 and it oscillates infinitely if x≤-1.
