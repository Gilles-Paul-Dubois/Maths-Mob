The following result says that we can integrate a F.T.E. term by term. More specifically:

théorème 1
Soit f une fonction définie et dérivable au voisinage de x0, et telle que la dérivée f'(x) possède un D.L. d'ordre n au voisinage de x0.
f'(x)=a0+a1(x-x0) + ... +an(x-x0)n+o((x-x0)n).
Dans ces conditions f possède un D.L. d'ordre n+1 en x0, disons
f(x)=b0+b1(x-x0)+b2(x-x0)2 + ... + bn(x-x0)n+bn+1(x-x0)n+1 + o((x-x0)n+1)
où
b0=f(x0)
bk=ak-1/k pour 1 ≤ k ≤n+1
theorem 1
Let f be a function defined and differentiable in the neighborhood of x0, and such that the derivative f'(x) has a F.T.E. of order n in the neighborhood of x0.
f'(x)=a0+a1(x-x0) + ... +an (x-x0)n+o((x-x0)n).

f(x)=b0+b1(x-x0)+b2(x-x 0)2 + ... + bn(x-x0)n+b< sub>n+1 (x-x0)n+1 + o((x-x0)n+1 )

bk=ak-1/k for 1 ≤ k≤n+1

proof

This time again we will give the proof in the particular case where x0=0, the general case being deduced by simple composition with a translation.
Suppose that f is differentiable in the neighborhood of 0 and that f' admits a F.T.E. of order n of the type f'(x)=P(x)+xnε1(x) where

\( \displaystyle {\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0}\epsilon_{1}}(x) = 0 \)

with P(x)=a0+a1x+ ... + anxn
Let's pose

\( \displaystyle Q(x) = a_{0}x + \frac{a_{1}}{2}x^{2} + ... + \frac{a_{n}}{n + 1}x^{ n+1} \)

We have Q'(x)=P(x) so f'(x)-Q'(x)=xnε1(x)

S'il est possible d'intégrer les D.L. il n'est pas possible, en revanche de les dériver.
Considérons par exemple la fonction définie au voisinage de 0 par :

\( \displaystyle f(x) = \left\{ \begin{matrix} {1 + x + x^{2}\sin\left( \frac{1}{x} \right){~~si~~}{x \neq 0}} \\ {1{~~si~~}x = 0} \\ \end{matrix} \right. \)

On a bien évidemment un D.L. du premier ordre f(x)=1+x+o(x)
f est dérivable en tout point f'(0)=0 et

\( \displaystyle f'(x) = 1 + 2x\sin\left( \frac{1}{x} \right) - \cos\left( \frac{1}{x} \right) \)

Mais on n'a pas de D.L. en 0 pour f' puisque f' n'est pas continue en 0.

If it is possible to integrate the F.T.E. it is not possible, on the other hand to derive them.
Consider for example the function defined near 0 by:

\( \displaystyle f(x) = \left\{ \begin{matrix} {1 + x + x^{2}\sin\left( \frac{1}{x} \right){~~if~~}{x \neq 0}} \\ {1{~~if~~}x = 0} \\ \end{matrix} \right. \)

We obviously have a first order F.T.E. f(x)=1+x+o(x)
f is differentiable at any point f'(0)=0 and

\( \displaystyle f'(x) = 1 + 2x\sin\left( \frac{1}{x} \right) - \cos\left( \frac{1}{x} \right) \)

But we don't have a F.T.E. at 0 for f' since f' is not continuous at 0.
