Definition

Reading this page assumes a prerequisite. It is necessary to know the definition of power series and some of their properties relating to convergence.
You can find all the necessary information .
définition 1
On dit que la fonction f définie au voisinage de x0 est 'développable en série entière en x0' (ou encore 'analytique' en x0), s'il existe une suite de coefficients réels a0, a1, .., an, ..., telle que :

\( \displaystyle f(x) = {\sum\limits_{n = 0}^{\infty}a_{n}\left( {x - x_{0}} \right)^{n}} \)

definition 1
We say that the function f defined in the neighborhood of x0 is 'expandable as a power series at x0' (or else 'analytical' at x0), if there exists a sequence of real coefficients a0 , a1, .., an, ..., such that:

\( \displaystyle f(x) = {\sum\limits_{n = 0}^{\infty}a_{n}\left( {x - x_{0}} \right)^{n}} \)

théorème 1
Il résulte de cette définition, ainsi que des propriétés des séries entières, que si f est analytique en x0, f possède en ce point des D.L. d'ordre n, pour n entier quelconque et que la suite (an)n ∈ ℕ est unique, que f est indéfiniment dérivable en x0 et que les coefficients an sont donnés par :

\( \displaystyle a_{n} = \frac{f^{(n)}\left( x_{0} \right)}{n!} \)

theorem 1
It follows from this definition, as well as from the properties of power series, that if f is analytic at x0, f has at this point F.T.E.s of order n, for any integer n and that the sequence (an)n ∈ ℕ is unique, that f is indefinitely differentiable at x0 and that the coefficients an are given by:

\( \displaystyle a_{n} = \frac{f^{(n)}\left( x_{0} \right)}{n!} \)

The expansions into power series of the usual functions at the origin will be obtained from their originally Finite Taylor Expansions collected in .
The radius of convergence, denoted R, of the series will be obtained by upper bounds for the remainder, of the type of the one presented in .
