Montrer que l'intégrale \( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi /2}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx \) est définie.

aide

Remarquer qu'une primitive de \( \displaystyle \frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}} \) sur ]0,π/2[ est \( \displaystyle \sqrt{sin(2x)} \)

solution

En utilisant la remarque faite dans l'aide, si 0 < a ≤ b < π/2, on a

\( \displaystyle \int_{a}^{b}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx=\left [\sqrt{sin(x)} \right ]_{a}^{b}=sin(2b)-sin(2a) \)

Il en résulte que

\( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi /2}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx=\lim_{b \to \pi /2-}\sqrt{sin(2b)}-\lim_{a \to 0+}\sqrt{sin(2a)}=0-0=0 \)

Show that the integral \( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi /2}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx \) exists.

hint

Notice that an antiderivative of \( \displaystyle \frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}} \) on ]0,π/2[ is \( \displaystyle \sqrt{sin(2x)} \)

solution

Using the indfication given in the help, if 0 < a ≤ b < π/2, we have

\( \displaystyle \int_{a}^{b}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx=\left [\sqrt{sin(x)} \right ]_{a}^{b}=sin(2b)-sin(2a) \)

and as a result

\( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi /2}\frac{cos(2x)}{\sqrt{sin(2x)}}dx=\lim_{b \to \pi /2-}\sqrt{sin(2b)}-\lim_{a \to 0+}\sqrt{sin(2a)}=0-0=0 \)
