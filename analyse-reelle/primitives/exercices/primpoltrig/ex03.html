Trouver une primitive de sin2xcos3x

aide

Commencer par linéariser.

solution

\( \displaystyle sin^{2}(x)cos^{3}(x)=\left ( 1-cos^{2}(x) \right )cos^{3}(x)=cos^{3}(x)-cos^{5}(x) \)

\( \displaystyle =\left ( \frac{3cos(x)}{4}+\frac{cos(3x)}{4} \right )-\left ( \frac{5cos(x)}{8}+\frac{5cos(3x)}{16} +\frac{cos(5x)}{16}\right ) \)

\( \displaystyle =\frac{cos(x)}{8}-\frac{cos(3x)}{16}-\frac{cos(5x)}{16} \)

Primitive :

\( \displaystyle \frac{sin(x)}{8}-\frac{sin(3x)}{48}-\frac{sin(5x)}{80}+K \)
