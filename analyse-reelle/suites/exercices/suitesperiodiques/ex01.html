We place ourselves in the vector space S of sequences of real numbers. (see ).
We denote by Sk the set of periodic sequences with period k.
h and k denoting two nonzero integers, we denote by h∧k their .
We denote by P the set of periodic sequences.

  1. Show that P is a vector subspace of S.
  2. Show that for any value of k, Skis a subspace of P.
  3. Are all the elements of Skof period k?
  4. Show that Pk∩Ph⊆ Pk∧h.
  5. Do we still have equality?

solution

  1. Let u be periodic with period k and v be periodic with period h then if w=u+v it is obvious that wn+kh=wn∀n, which proves that w is periodic.
  2. If u ∈ Skand v∈ S kthen u+v ∈ S kand ∀λ λu ∈ Sk.
  3. No, Sk can contain sequences the period of which is a divisor of k.
  4. The relations un+k=un∀n and un+h=un∀n necessarily entail un+p=un∀n whenever p is a common multiple of h and k so especially when p=h∧k.
  5. No, taking for example k=2 and h=4, we have S2∩S4=S2 and 2∧4=4
