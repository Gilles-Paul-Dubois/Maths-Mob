Montrer que la suite \( \frac{n\times sin(n!)}{1+n^{2}} \) est bornée.

aide

Utiliser l'inégalité |sin(x)| ≤1

solution

\( u_{n}= \frac{n\times sin(n!)}{1+n^{2}} \) on a donc:
\( \left| u_{n}\right|\leqslant \frac{n}{1+n^{2}}=\frac{1}{n+1/n} \)
Pour n ≥ 1 \( \frac{1}{n+1/n}\leqslant \frac{1}{2} \)
Comme u0=0 on a toujours |un| ≤ 1/2

Show that the sequence \( \frac{n\times sin(n!)}{1+n^{2}} \) is bounded.

hint

Use the inequality |sin(x)| ≤1

solution

If \( u_{n}= \frac{n\times sin(n!)}{1+n^{2}} \) we have :
\( \left| u_{n}\right|\leqslant \frac{n}{1+n^{2}}=\frac{1}{n+1/n} \)
For n ≥ 1 \( \frac{1}{n+1/n}\leqslant \frac{1}{2} \)
Since u0=0 we always have |un| ≤ 1/2
