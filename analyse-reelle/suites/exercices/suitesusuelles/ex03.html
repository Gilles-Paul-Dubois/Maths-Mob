Calculer

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}} \)

solution

\( \displaystyle \frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}=\frac{2}{n+1} \)

D'après le cours :

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{2}{n+1}=0 \)

Donc

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}=0 \)

Il existe donc un entier N tel que pour tout n>N on ait:

\( \displaystyle \frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}< \frac{1}{2} \)

Nous en déduisons par récurrence:

\( \displaystyle u_{N+k}< \left ( \frac{1}{2} \right )^{k}u_{N} \)

Et comme (1/2)k →0 quand k→∞ puisque 1/2<1
Il en résulte que:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }u_{n}=0 \)

Compute

solution

\( \displaystyle \frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}=\frac{2}{n+1} \)

From the course :

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{2}{n+1}=0 \)

Hence

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}=0 \)

So we can find a natural N such that for every n>N we have:

\( \displaystyle \frac{u_{n+1}}{u_{n}}< \frac{1}{2} \)

By induction, we conclude :

\( \displaystyle u_{N+k}< \left ( \frac{1}{2} \right )^{k}u_{N} \)

And since (1/2)k →0 when k→∞ since 1/2<1
We can conclude that :

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }u_{n}=0 \)
