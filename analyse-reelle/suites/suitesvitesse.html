Définitions

We note that the limit is in any interval ]u2n,u2n-1[ of length 1/(2n+1).

We note however that the length of these intervals does not decrease in a given ratio because the ratio of the lengths of these intervals tends towards 1.
This is what we call a case of 'slow convergence' .
After 80000 iterations we only have 4 exact decimals. After 5000 we already had 3.

As before, from a certain rank N, we have k1n2<0 by multiplication we will have:

If p is odd

\( \displaystyle k_{1}^{p}\leqslant \frac{u_{n+p+1}-u_{n+p}}{u_{n+1}-u_{p}}\leqslant k_{2}^{p} \)

and if p is even

\( \displaystyle k_{2}^{p}\leqslant \frac{u_{n+p+1}-u_{n+p}}{u_{n+1}-u_{p}}\leqslant k_{1}^{p} \)

By summation, it then comes if n>N and if p is odd

\( \displaystyle 1+k_{1}+k_{2}^{2}+k_{1}^{3}+...+k_{2}^{p-1}+k_{1}^{p}\leqslant \frac{u_{n+p+1}-u_{n}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \leqslant 1+k_{2}+k_{1}^{2}+k_{2}^{3}+...+k_{1}^{p-1}+k_{2}^{p} \)

We therefore obtain by leaving n fixed and by making p tend towards +∞

\( \displaystyle 1+\frac{k_{1}}{1-k_{1}^{2}}+\frac{k_{2}^{2}}{1-k_{2}^{2}}\leqslant \frac{a-u_{n}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \leqslant 1+\frac{k_{2}}{1-k_{2}^{2}}+\frac{k_{1}^{2}}{1-k_{1}^{2}} \)

But k1 and k2 can be chosen as close to k as desired.
But then the 2 expressions that frame the quotient

\( \displaystyle \frac{a-u_{n}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \)

Are as close to 1/(1-k) as desired.
It follows that:
    \( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{a-u_{n}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}}=\frac{1}{1-k} \)
So that :

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\frac{u_{n+1}-a}{u_{n}-a}=k \)

Case k=0

Let ε be a given real satisfying 0<ε<1
There thus exists an integer N such that for n>N we have: |qn|<ε
We therefore have for n>N and any integer p:

\( \displaystyle \left|\frac{u_{n+p+1}-u_{n+p}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \right|\leqslant \varepsilon ^{p} \)

Summing from p=1, it comes:

\( \displaystyle \left|\frac{u_{n+p+1}-u_{n+1}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \right|\leqslant \varepsilon +...+\varepsilon ^{p} \)

By letting p tend to +∞ we deduce:

\( \displaystyle \left|\frac{a-u_{n+1}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}} \right|\leqslant \frac{\varepsilon }{1-\varepsilon } \)

And so, as ε is arbitrarily small:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\left| \frac{a-u_{n+1}}{u_{n+1}-u_{n}}\right|=0_{+} \)

So we get by inversion:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\left|\frac{ u_{n}-a}{u_{n+1}-a}-1\right|=+\infty \)

From where we derive:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\left|\frac{ u_{n}-a}{u_{n+1}-a}\right|=+\infty \)

and by a new inversion:

\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty }\left|\frac{ u_{n+1}-a}{u_{n}-a}\right|=0_{+} \)
