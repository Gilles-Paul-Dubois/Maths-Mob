Calculer la somme de la série de terme général:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{1}{n(n+1)(n+2)} \)

défini pour n≥1.

aide

Trouver des constantes a,b,c telles que:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{a}{n}+\frac{b}{n+1}+\frac{c}{n+2} \)

solution

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{0,5}{n}+\frac{-1}{n+1}+\frac{0,5}{n+2} \)

Par sommation les groupes s'annulent par groupes de 3 et il reste finalement:
sn=(a/1+b/2+a/2)+ (c/(n+2)+b/(n+1)+c/(n+1))
Le terme c/(n+2)+b/(n+1)+c/(n+1) tend vers 0.
Il reste donc:
S=a/1+b/2+a/2=1/4

Calculate the sum of the series with general term:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{1}{n(n+1)(n+2)} \)

defined for n≥1.

hint

Find constants a,b,c such as:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{a}{n}+\frac{b}{n+1}+\frac{c}{n+2} \)

solution

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{0.5}{n}+\frac{-1}{n+1}+\frac{0.5}{n+2} \)

By summation the groups cancel each other out by groups of 3 and there is finally left :
sn=(a/1+b/2+a/2)+ (c/(n+2)+b/(n+1)+c/(n+1))
The term c/(n+2)+b/(n+1)+c/(n+1) tends to 0.
So it remains:
S=a/1+b/2+a/2=1/4
