Explanation of the method

Suppose that we have to study the convergence of a series (s,u) whose general term is defined say for n≥1 and that we notice that there exists a sequence (vn ) (n≥0) such that:
un=vn-vn-1 for all n≥1
Note that:
s1=u1=v1-v0
s2=u1+u2=(v2 - v1 )+( v1 -v0)= v2-v0 (cancellation or 'telescoping' of v1 terms ).
s3=(v3 -v2 )+( v2 -v1 )+( v1 -v0)=v3-v0 (telescoping terms v1,v2).
And more generally by induction on n:
sn=vn-v0.
So that:
propriété 1
La série (s,u) converge si et seulement si la suite (vn) converge dans ce cas on a S=a-v0 où a=limn→∞vn.
property 1
The series (s,u) converges if and only if the sequence (vn) converges in this case we have S=a-v0 where a=limn&rarr ;∞vn.

Exemple traité

Etudions la série (s,u) de terme général:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{1}{n(n+1)} \)

On a un=vn-vn-1
où \( \displaystyle v_{n}=\frac{-1}{n+1} \)
Comme limn→∞vn=0, on obtient S=-v0=1

Processed example

Let us study the series (s,u) of general term:

\( \displaystyle u_{n}=\frac{1}{n(n+1)} \)

We have un=vn-vn-1
where \( \displaystyle v_{n}=\frac{-1}{n+1} \)
As limn→∞vn=0, we get S=-v0=1
