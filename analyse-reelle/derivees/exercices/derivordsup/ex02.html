On suppose f définie sur un intervalle [a,b] et vérifiant:
f(a)=f'(a)=f"(a)= .... =f(n+1)(a)=0 et f(b)=0
Montrer que dans ces conditions ∃ c ∈ ]a,b[ tel que f(n)(c)=0.

aide

Raisonner par récurrence sur n.

solution

Puisque f(a)=f(b)=0, il existe un réel c1∈ ]a,b[ tel que f'(c1)=0 (Rolle)
Puisque f'(a)=0 et f'(c1)=0 Il existe c2 ∈ ]a,c1[ tel que f"(c2)=0 (Rolle encore)
Puisque f"(a)=f"(c2)=0 il existe c3 ∈ ]a,c2[ tel que f(3)(c3)=0
et ainsi de suite ....

We suppose f defined on an interval [a,b] and satisfying:
f(a)=f'(a)=f"(a)= .... =f(n+1)(a)=0 and f(b)=0
Show that under these conditions ∃ c ∈ ]a,b[ such that f(n)(c)=0.

hint

Reason by induction over n.

solution

Since f(a)=f(b)=0, there exists a real c1∈ ]a,b[ such that f'(c1)=0 (Rolle)
Since f'(a)=0 and f'(c1)=0 There is c2 ∈ ]a,c1[ such that f"(c2)=0 (Roll again)
Since f"(a)=f"(c2)=0 there is c3 ∈ ]a,c2[ such that f(3)(c3)=0
and so on....
